Discover Sri Lanka's Sacred Hill Capital

Kandy, nestled among misty green hills at 500 metres elevation, was the last royal capital of the Sinhalese kings. At its heart lies the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa) — one of Buddhism's holiest pilgrimage sites, housing a tooth of the Buddha himself.

Beyond the temple, Kandy offers the magnificent Peradeniya Royal Botanic Gardens (60+ hectares of tropical flora), vibrant Kandyan dance performances, spice gardens, and tea factory visits in the surrounding hills. Our private tours let you experience it all at your own pace.

Sample 2-Day Itinerary

Day1

Arrival → Kandy via Pinnawala

Pickup from Colombo and drive to Kandy. En route, visit Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage to watch elephants bathe in the river. Afternoon stop at a spice garden in Matale. Evening attend the Kandyan Cultural Dance show and visit the Temple of the Tooth for the evening puja ceremony.

Day2

Peradeniya Gardens & Tea Country

Morning at Peradeniya Royal Botanic Gardens — walk among 4,000+ plant species including giant bamboo and the orchid house. Afternoon drive to a highland tea estate for a factory tour and guided tasting. Return via Bahirawakanda for panoramic views of Kandy city.
